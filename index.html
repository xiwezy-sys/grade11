End-of-Summer Goals

  • Write clear, arguable, precise thesis statements.
  • Build paragraphs with claim, evidence, analysis, and extension.
  • Understand the scoring logic behind SAQ, DBQ, and LEQ responses.
  • Complete one polished academic essay and two early UC PIQ drafts.

SAQ: Precision

The goal is to answer exactly what is asked. Most SAQ parts can be answered in 3-5 focused sentences.

  1. A Answer the prompt directly.
  2. C Cite a specific historical fact.
  3. E Explain how the fact answers the prompt.

DBQ: Document-Based Argument

The goal is to use documents to build a historical argument, not to summarize each document one by one.

  1. Read the prompt and identify the reasoning skill.
  2. Group documents by argument.
  3. Write a thesis, context, and outside evidence.
  4. Use sourcing/HIPP for selected documents.

LEQ: Knowledge-Based Argument

Without documents, the writer must organize historical knowledge into a clear line of reasoning.

  1. Causation: causes and effects.
  2. Comparison: similarities and differences.
  3. CCOT: continuity and change over time.

Academic Essay Framework

Thesis Topic Sentence Evidence Analysis Complexity

The goal is not simply to write more. The goal is to make each paragraph move the argument forward: from a correct point to a meaningful claim, and from evidence placement to evidence explanation.

Application Essay Preparation

UC PIQs are direct and action-based; Common App writing is more narrative and voice-driven. Before senior year, the best preparation is building a bank of specific, reflective stories.

Revision Priorities

  1. Revise the thesis first: Is it specific, arguable, and appropriately scoped?
  2. Revise paragraph structure: Does each paragraph make one clear move?
  3. Revise evidence: Is there specific textual or historical support?
  4. Revise analysis: Does the writer explain why the evidence matters?
  5. Revise sentences last: clarity, accuracy, and control.

Helpful Parent Questions

  • Which sentence is your thesis?
  • What is the main point of this paragraph?
  • Why does this evidence prove your claim?
  • Which paragraph feels weakest? Why?
  • If you could revise only one thing, what would you revise?

What to Avoid

  • Do not focus only on grammar and spelling.
  • Do not rewrite the student's natural voice into an adult voice.
  • Do not encourage formulaic templates or inflated vocabulary.
  • Do not expect every draft to be perfect; look for visible growth.
